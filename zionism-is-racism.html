UN slogan / racism charge / legitimacy attack

Zionism Is Racism

Definition

“Zionism is racism” is a delegitimizing slogan that defines Jewish national self-determination as racial domination. It does not merely criticize an Israeli government or policy. It declares the Jewish national movement guilty by nature.

The phrase became internationally famous through UN General Assembly Resolution 3379 in 1975, which determined that Zionism was a form of racism and racial discrimination. The General Assembly revoked that resolution in 1991. The slogan remained.

Its central error is categorical. Zionism is the national movement of the Jewish people. It can be debated, criticized, defended, divided by school and party, and judged by the actions of the state it helped create. But calling it racism as such turns Jewish peoplehood into a crime.

Common Usage

The slogan appears in protest chants, campus resolutions, boycott campaigns, speeches, posters, and social media. It is often paired with apartheid, colonialism, genocide, and white supremacy. The goal is to make Zionism morally untouchable.

In practice, the slogan usually avoids Israel’s actual legal and civic record. Arab citizens of Israel vote, serve in the Knesset, sit on courts, practice medicine, teach in universities, and participate in public life. Israel has inequalities and unresolved discrimination, but those facts do not equal a doctrine of racial exclusion.

The slogan also ignores the diversity of Jews. A movement that became home to Jews from Europe, the Middle East, North Africa, Ethiopia, the former Soviet Union, and elsewhere cannot be reduced honestly to white racial supremacy.

Implied Meaning

The implied meaning is that the Jewish state is racist in its essence, not merely flawed in its policies. If the essence is racist, reform is insufficient. The remedy becomes dismantlement.

That is why the phrase is so powerful. It moves the debate away from borders, settlements, minority rights, war, security, or Palestinian statehood. It asks the audience to condemn Zionism before any specific policy is considered.

Origin / Lineage

The phrase grew out of Cold War diplomacy. Soviet, Arab-bloc, and allied states worked to isolate Israel after 1967 by linking Zionism to colonialism, racism, imperialism, and South African apartheid. UN General Assembly Resolution 3151G in 1973 condemned an “unholy alliance” between Portuguese colonialism, South African racism, Zionism, and Israeli imperialism.

Resolution 3379 followed in 1975 and gave the campaign its most famous formula. It was part of a wider diplomatic effort to strip Israel of legitimacy by defining the Jewish national movement as racism. In 1991, Resolution 46/86 revoked 3379.

Function

The slogan functions as a verdict before evidence. Once Zionism is defined as racism, Jewish self-determination no longer needs to be debated. It only needs to be opposed.

It also makes ordinary Jewish attachment to Israel suspect. A diaspora Jew who supports Israel’s existence can be described as defending racism, even if that person supports Palestinian rights, opposes settlements, or criticizes Israeli governments.

Notes

1. Resolution 3379 was adopted in 1975 and revoked in 1991.

2. The repeal removed the UN resolution, not the slogan from activist language.

3. Jewish self-determination is not racial supremacy. It is a national claim by a people with a long history, religion, language, and collective memory.

Responses / Criticisms

It criminalizes Jewish peoplehood. Zionism is not the claim that Jews are racially superior. It is the claim that Jews are a people entitled to national self-determination.

It erases the actual record. Israel has Arab citizens with voting rights and public offices. That does not make Israel perfect. It does make the racism slogan too blunt to be honest.

It imports a dismantlement logic. If Zionism is racism, then the Jewish state is not a state with problems; it is a racist structure to be ended. That is why the slogan is not normal policy criticism.
