Glossary A–Z

A directory of terms, slogans, and accusations in modern antizionism.

ABCEFGHIJMNOPSZ

A

  • Anti-Zionism, 1880–1947Early religious, socialist, liberal, and assimilationist objections before Israel existed.
  • Anti-Zionism, 1948–October 6, 2023How opposition to Jewish statehood changed after Israel’s founding and before October 7.
  • Antizionism, October 7, 2023–PresentHow antizionism after October 7 moved into a demand to isolate Jewish national life.
  • ApartheidThe term turns a national conflict into a claim of racial caste.
  • Apartheid WallThe phrase turns a disputed security structure into proof of racial supremacy.

B

  • Blood on Your HandsThe slogan revives old depictions of the blood libel.
  • Boycott, Divestment, Sanctions (BDS)The movement turns contact with Israel into complicity.
  • Butcher(s) of GazaThe phrase misconstrues wartime judgment as deliberate slaughter.
  • By Any Means NecessaryThe phrase blurs the line between resistance and permission for atrocity.

C

  • Child KillersThe term implies that the IDF deliberately kills children.
  • Collective PunishmentThe term shifts military action into a claim of intentional civilian suffering.

E

  • End the BlockadeThe slogan removes Hamas rule and weapons smuggling from the account.
  • End the OccupationThe phrase can mean withdrawal from 1967 territories—or the undoing of the state of Israel itself.
  • EthnostateThe term recasts Jewish self-determination as ethnic supremacy.

F

  • Free PalestineThe phrase can mean rights, statehood, or the disappearance of the state of Israel.
  • From the River to the SeaThe slogan names the whole land and leaves no room for the state of Israel.

G

  • Genocide / GenocidalThe word turns a war against Hamas into an accusation of exterminationist intent.
  • Globalize the IntifadaThe phrase points to the First and Second Intifadas and carries that violence into Jewish life abroad.

H

  • HasbaraThe term turns pro-Israel argument into bad-faith manipulation.

I

  • Imperial StateThe label makes Israel an empire while obscuring Jewish vulnerability and indigeneity.

J

  • Jewish SupremacyThe phrase translates Jewish national survival into domination.

M

  • Modern-Day NazisThe comparison turns Jews into heirs of their destroyers.

N

  • Nazi StateThe phrase uses the Holocaust’s weight against the Jewish state.

O

  • Open-Air PrisonThe phrase turns Gaza into a single Israeli-made cage.

P

  • PinkwashingThe term treats Israeli liberalism as a disguise for oppression.

S

  • Settler-ColonialismThe category casts Zionism as foreign implantation rather than Jewish return.
  • Stop the GenocideThe phrase turns a war against Hamas into a claim of extermination.

Z

  • ZionismThe term as recast after October 7 inside contemporary antizionist vocabulary.
