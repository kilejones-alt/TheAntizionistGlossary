Glossary A–Z

A directory of terms, slogans, and accusations in modern antizionism.

ABCDEFGHIJLMNOPRSWZ

A

  • Anti-Zionism, 1880–1947Early religious, civic, socialist, and Arab objections before Jewish sovereignty.
  • Anti-Zionism, 1948–October 6, 2023The post-state campaign against Israel’s legitimacy before the October 7 massacres.
  • Antizionism, October 7, 2023–PresentThe contemporary mass movement exposed and accelerated after October 7.
  • ApartheidThe term turns a national conflict into a claim of racial caste.
  • Apartheid WallThe phrase turns a disputed security structure into proof of racial supremacy.

B

  • Blood on Your HandsThe slogan revives old depictions of the blood libel.
  • Boycott, Divestment, Sanctions (BDS)The movement turns contact with Israel into complicity.
  • Butcher(s) of GazaThe phrase misconstrues wartime judgment as deliberate slaughter.
  • By Any Means NecessaryThe phrase blurs the line between resistance and permission for atrocity.

C

  • Ceasefire NowThe slogan can demand Israeli restraint while leaving Hamas and the hostages unnamed.
  • Child KillersThe term implies that the IDF deliberately kills children.
  • Collective PunishmentThe term shifts military action into a claim of intentional civilian suffering.
  • ColonizerThe label casts Jews as foreign invaders and pushes Jewish history out of view.
  • ComplicityThe word turns contact with Israelis or pro-Israel Jews into shared guilt.

D

  • Death to the IDFThe phrase turns hatred of Israel’s army into a public wish for dead soldiers.
  • DecolonizationThe term can turn Jewish sovereignty itself into the colonial object to be undone.

E

  • End the BlockadeThe slogan removes Hamas rule and weapons smuggling from the account.
  • End the OccupationThe phrase can mean withdrawal from 1967 territories—or the undoing of the state of Israel itself.
  • Ethnic CleansingThe phrase recasts Israel’s founding and wartime conduct as a permanent project of removal.
  • EthnostateThe term recasts Jewish self-determination as ethnic supremacy.

F

  • Free PalestineThe phrase can mean rights, statehood, or the disappearance of the state of Israel.
  • From the River to the SeaThe slogan names the whole land and leaves no room for the state of Israel.

G

  • GenocideThe word turns a war against Hamas into an accusation of Jewish exterminationist intent.
  • Globalize the IntifadaThe phrase points to the First and Second Intifadas and carries that violence into Jewish life abroad.

H

  • HasbaraThe term turns pro-Israel argument into bad-faith manipulation.

I

  • Imperial StateThe label makes Israel an empire while obscuring Jewish vulnerability and indigeneity.
  • IntifadaThe word carries the memory of uprisings that included attacks on Israeli civilians.
  • Israeli War MachineThe phrase depicts Israel as a machine built for violence.

J

  • Jewish SupremacyThe phrase translates Jewish national survival into domination.

L

  • LiberationThe word can mean Palestinian freedom or a land emptied of Israel.

M

  • Modern-Day NazisThe comparison turns Jews into heirs of their destroyers.

N

  • Nazi StateThe phrase uses the Holocaust’s weight against the Jewish state.
  • NormalizationThe term condemns ordinary contact with Israelis or Zionists as betrayal.

O

  • OccupationThe term can name the post-1967 territories, or expand to describe Israel itself as the occupation.
  • Open-Air PrisonThe phrase turns Gaza into a single Israeli-made cage.

P

  • PinkwashingThe term treats Israeli liberalism as a disguise for oppression.

R

  • ResistanceThe word can turn terrorism against Israeli civilians into a sanctified political category.

S

  • Settler-ColonialismThe category casts Zionism as foreign implantation rather than Jewish return.
  • Stop the GenocideThe phrase turns a war against Hamas into a claim of extermination.

W

  • White SettlerThe phrase erases Mizrahi, Sephardi, Ethiopian, and other Jewish histories.

Z

  • Zio / ZionaziThe terms blur Zionist into a stigmatized object of contempt.
  • Zionism Is RacismThe phrase declares Jewish national life racist by definition.
  • Zionism, pre-stateJewish national revival before sovereign statehood.
  • Zionism, 1948–October 6, 2023Jewish self-determination expressed through the state of Israel before October 7.
  • Zionism, October 7, 2023–PresentThe term as recast after October 7 inside contemporary antizionist vocabulary.
  • Zionist EntityThe phrase refuses to name Israel and substitutes a term that denies ordinary state legitimacy.
